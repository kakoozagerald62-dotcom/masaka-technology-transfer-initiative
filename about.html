Who We Are

MTTI (Masaka Technology Transfer Initiative) is a Christ-centered, farmer-focused, non-profit organization based in Kyanamukaaka Town Council, Masaka District, Uganda. We are dedicated to transforming subsistence farming into sustainable agribusiness and empowering rural farmers with knowledge and tools for lasting change.

Our Vision

To transform traditional subsistence farming into agribusiness to ensure decent lives for farmers and prepare for a future where technology uplifts rural livelihoods.

Our Mission

To provide rural smallholder farmers with convenient, easily accessible agribusiness information to start, build, and grow enterprises — improving income, employment, and food security.

Our Core Values

Teamwork

We believe in collective effort to achieve shared goals.

Integrity

We uphold transparency, honesty, and accountability.

Service

We are committed to servant leadership rooted in faith.

Innovation

We embrace technology and creative problem-solving.

Stewardship

We responsibly manage resources for God’s glory and future generations.

Hard Work & Diligence

We put in the effort required to ensure successful crop yields.

Overall Goal

To increase rural incomes and household food and nutrition security in line with Uganda’s national agricultural development strategies.

Our Future

We envision a self-sustaining, Christ-centered movement of empowered farmers lifting communities from poverty to opportunity. MTTI aims to be a model for agricultural transformation in Uganda — transparent, effective, and rooted in shared values.
